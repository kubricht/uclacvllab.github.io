Jewelry Heist

Imagine that you are a thief preparing for a heist along with some fellow team members. Your objective is to sneak into a museum and steal a valuable piece of jewelry that is stored inside of a locked box.


To prepare for the heist, the engineer on your team has created a replica of the locked box, as well as the unlocking mechanism used to open it. The purpose of the replica is to provide a tool for you (the thief) to practice on.

Users attempting to unlock the box may perform three actions to both unlock and open the box.
In other words, two actions must be used to unlock the box, and one action must be used to open the box.

Remember: Three actions define a single attempt.

Unfortunately, the engineer on your team (who designed the replica) failed several classes in college. Therefore, the replica of the box and locking mechanism do not always operate how they are supposed to.
Since the replica is faulty (because of bad engineering), even the correct button combinations will sometimes fail to unlock the box during an attempt.


In addition, incorrect combinations will sometimes unlock the box. Thus, to figure out the locking mechanism, it is necessary to try out different combinations several times.
